Welcome to Kiwi-Dú

Kiwi-Dú provides a modern platform for:

  • Language learning, Chinese/English
  • Cultural comprehension
  • Online resources
  • Core values
  • Teachables
  • New Zealand & China’s pioneering partnership

Confidence knowing a language can build as we grow and ‘experience’ meaning, through illustrated stories. Kiwi-Dú helps strengthen English and Chinese language learning with children’s books that have tangible, educational value. Read, listen, look, and gain confidence!

Find out how kids you love can explore thoughtful adventures, core values and cultural appreciation with KiwiReads bilingual Chinese/English childrens books.

About Kiwi-Dú

Kiwi-Dú Directors Ron and Heather Vail are also the primary storybook authors and illustrators. Dr. Ron Vail is a medical doctor with a general practice specialty. He enjoys illustrating and editing children's books. Heather is an educator with a global studies focus. She has written for newspapers and academic journals and now enjoys writing stories for children. Ron and Heather have traveled through many countries (including China) and now live and work in New Zealand.

Their adult children, Laleyna and Julian, are knowledgeable in psychology, youth, business, culture and parenting. Julian and Laleyna also write and illustrate for Kiwi-Dú. Hopefully they can visit China some day soon!

Chinese translation is by Min Zhang, who lives in China and once-upon-a-time worked in New Zealand at a polytechnic. Min's knowledge of language, education, culture and children's literature adds a robust grace to every story.

It is with aroha - love - that Kiwi-Dú's bilingual children’s book series are imagined, created, put together and made for the emerging global population. Kiwi-Dú aims to help us all deepen our understandings for a happy, successful and peaceful future.

Children Books

Series Age group Defining characteristics
Sunshine baby books 0-3 Large format picture book, dazzling illustrations, engages creativity, warm, happy feelings
Comparable situations 3-6 Picture book, current everyday Chinese & Western scenarios depicted side-by-side, vocabulary
Dragon Chat & friends 5-8 Picture book, understanding differences, confidence, friendship-building, storytelling
Healthy Habit Heroes 5-10 Picture book, engaging read, core values for youngsters, models social/personal good behavior
Eight Spirit Animals 8-13 Chapter book, stories, fantasy and appreciation of cultural legends, trust, friendship, reading skills

Large format picture books and young teen chapter books will have both Chinese and English text. Accompanying online portals provide audio in both languages so readers can hear the words they see. Web links are provided for teaching resources: key words lists, themes and suggested reader engagement tips/ideas.

Sample story synopsis and illustration from the Spirit Animals series:

One morning Fan is awoken by a strange noise in her backyard, and soon finds, next to a pile of dirt surrounding a freshly-dug hole, a boy with long hair and a slab of greenstone hung around his neck.

Just before Fan screams for her Mom & Dad, an owl flutters onto the boy’s shoulder, tilts its head and stares at her with curious eyes.

Fan stares back, as if the owl is trying to tell her something. ‘Kia ora, my name is Tane!’ the boy says. Tane convinces the curious Fan to slide through the hole back to his home in New Zealand.

Their exciting adventure includes a visit to the Marae, where Nana feeds everyone in a traditional Maori setting. Nana and Fan say goodbye with aroha (love).

Sample story synopsis, excerpt and illustrations from the Sunshine Baby series:

Baby wakes up to the morning sun and follows it around the house and garden throughout the day.

The sun illuminates the wonders of the world, its comfort and warmth, as well as connectivity to environment. This first in the series counts in rhyming format from 1 to 10.

News

Two Press Releases listed

For release 2020

A. Kiwi-Dú is set to bring their bilingual children’s books series to China in July, 2020. The Directors plan to meet with Chinese publishers and agents to discuss how to best put these exclusive learning tools into the hands of Chinese families. They will discuss production, distribution and online content platforms. Kiwi-Dú and a designated publisher will review allocation to stores, libraries and learning establishments within China. “The bilingual, English/Chinese language children’s books that we have will be a unique initiative that I have not seen in China or New Zealand before, and with it we hope to build successful bridges between the two countries who share the goals of educating our children,” Director Heather Vail said. Updates will be released following the visits and agreed upon outcomes.

B. 2020 as Year of the Rat – include Chinese Ambassador to New Zealand Wu Xi’s address that remarks on our countries’ combined pioneering spirit.
